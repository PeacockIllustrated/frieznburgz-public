Preparation

The Preparation section outlines all tasks that must be completed before opening and after closing. These duties are divided into Morning (Opening) Duties and Night Time (Closing) Duties to ensure that each shift starts and ends smoothly.

Morning Duties (Opening Preparation)

1. Preparing the Seating and Store Environment

  • Remove chairs from tables (since floors were cleaned the night before).
  • Open all window shutters and front shutters to allow natural light inside.
  • Turn on all TVs in the store so they are ready for customers. (See pg 30).
  • Clean all glass surfaces, including windows, the entrance door, and the door handle. (See pg 30).

2. Set up the Music

  • From 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM (breakfast) or 12:00 PM to 5:00 PM (regular): play House Chill Music.
  • After 5:00 PM, when the busy period starts, switch to Dance House Music.
  • Music volume should be comfortable for taking orders—background music only, not too loud.

3. Setting Up the Till and Checking Cash Float

  • Turn on the till system and make sure it's working.
  • Count the cash float and ensure it is exactly £150 at the start of the shift.
  • If the cash float is not £150, adjust it accordingly before starting service.

Night Time Duties (Closing Preparation)

1. Cleaning and Sanitizing the Work Area

  • Wipe down all surfaces, including tills, countertops, card machines, and order screens.
  • Sweep and mop the entire front area floor. Put chairs on top of tables to make mopping easier.
  • Empty all bins and replace garbage bags if full.

2. Restocking the Sauce Fridges

  • There are three sauce fridges: Front Small Fridge, Backup Small Fridge, and Large Back Fridge.
  • Closing Staff Responsibilities: Refill the front small fridge from the backup, then refill the backup from the large back fridge. Organize using the FIFO method.

Service

This section outlines the key responsibilities of front staff, focusing on customer interactions, order-taking, and essential operational tasks. The goal is to ensure efficient service and a welcoming atmosphere.

1. Customer Service

Front staff must ensure that every customer feels welcomed, valued, and satisfied.

Key Rules:

  • Always smile and maintain eye contact when greeting customers.
  • Never turn your back on a customer while they are waiting or speaking.
  • Use a warm and friendly tone when taking orders and assisting guests.
  • If the store is not busy, engage in light conversation to make them feel at home.
GOAL: Every customer should leave feeling like they had a great experience and want to come back!

2. Upselling Techniques

Upselling helps increase sales and ensures customers get the best experience. Every cashier must use the Sales Script naturally and friendly.

Sales Script

Greeting: “Hello! Welcome to Friez&Burgz. How's your day going?” (Smile and make eye contact).

Offering Extras: After a customer chooses a burger, ask: “Would you like to add some bacon or pastrami to your burger?”

Suggesting Sides: “Would you like any sides with your meal? We have fillets, fries, and corn bites.”

Offering Drinks & Desserts: “Would you like any pops, cheesecakes, milkshakes or coffees?”

Everyday Rules

To ensure smooth operations and a professional work environment, all staff must follow these essential everyday rules.

1. Mobile Phone Policy

  • Mobile phones must not be used during work hours except during your designated 20-minute break.
  • Your full focus should be on customers and tasks. Phone usage is monitored via security cameras.

2. Staff Meals Policy

  • Staff are entitled to one free meal and a can of pop per shift.
  • Cheesecakes and milkshakes are not included and must be paid for. Coffee and water are free.

3. Break Policy

  • Full-day shifts (12:00 PM – 9:00 PM): One 20-minute uninterrupted break.
  • Half-day shifts (4:00 PM – 9:00 PM): A quick 5-minute fresh air break is allowed during non-busy periods.
  • Leaving your post without approval is not permitted.

Store & Staff Performance Evaluation

This checklist is designed to ensure our stores operate at the highest standards. Evaluations are conducted weekly for store maintenance and daily for individual staff performance.

Evaluation System

  • Each store starts with 100 points.
  • If a point is completed perfectly, 0 points are deducted.
  • If a point does not meet the standard, 2 points are deducted.
  • If the same issue is found again, the deduction doubles (2 -> 4 -> 8).
  • Once corrected, the deduction resets to 0.

Final Score Interpretation:

  • 90-100 points: Excellent (Eligible for bonuses)
  • 80-89 points: Good (Minor issues)
  • 70-79 points: Needs Improvement (Requires attention)
  • Below 70 points: Unsatisfactory (Immediate corrective actions)

Disciplinary Actions & Accountability

We maintain high standards for service, cleanliness, teamwork, and professionalism. This section explains what happens if these standards are not met.

Types of Violations:

  • Customer Service Issues: Being rude, failing to greet customers, ignoring upselling.
  • Work Ethic & Performance: Failing to complete tasks, leaving duties unfinished, ignoring standards.
  • Violations of Company Policies: Using a mobile phone on duty, repeated lateness, mishandling inventory.
  • Serious Misconduct: Theft, intentional damage, aggression, disregarding allergen policies.

Disciplinary Action Process:

  1. Step 1: Verbal Warning - For minor infractions.
  2. Step 2: Written Warning - If the issue continues or for a more serious violation.
  3. Step 3: Final Written Warning - If no improvement is seen after a monitoring period.
  4. Step 4: Termination - For repeated offenses or serious misconduct.
